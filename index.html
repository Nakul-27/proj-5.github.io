What Cassava-Infecting Geminiviruses Can Tell Us About the Effects of the Scramble for Africa

Cassava-Infecting Geminiviruses are viruses that ``cause the most economic damage'' to the cassava plant Chikoti et. al. Cassava is a major staple food in the developing world - particularly Africa. It provides a basic diet for over half a billion people Wikipedia. The evolution of a virus (more strains means more evolutions) indicates ``a noncyclic change in the genetic characteristics of [the] virus'' Villareal. While this does not directly indicate (to my knowledge) that a virus is stronger and more resistent, it isn't farfetched to say that the more a virus evolves, there is potential for it to pose a threat to existing crops - in this case the Cassava plant.

The scramble for Africa was a phenomenon in which a few European countries colonized most of Africa and the middle east. This was at the height of their empires. It is no secret that this has had lasting ramifications on the African continent in all facets of life, from technological development to political instability. For this project, I'm curious as to the intersection between the scramble for Africa, Cassava-Infecting Geminiviruses and Hospital Infrastructure. While Cassava-Infecting Geminiviruses cannot cause direct illnesses, I am curious as to how African countries can take care of their people from a medical perspective - because as people go hungry they become more susceptible to illness (as malnutrition compromises the immune system). Hypothesis: I believe that the African Countries that were colonized by England or France will see the largest amount of cassava virus strains and most negative effect on their hospital infrastructure.


There were two datasets utilized:
  1. Real-time tracking of cassava-infecting geminiviruses in Africa (Nextstrain.org) This was newick tree data and metadata.
  2. Global Health Observatory Data Repository (Africa Region) - Health infrastructure Data by Country This was the total density of hospitals per 100,000 people. Some countries were unknown. Tooltips indicate this.
Note: The color system for this is based off the colonizing country, not the colonized country, and only few (16 specifically) African countries were plotted because of the constraints of the Nextstrain dataset and desire for continuity.
The countries that were plotted are: Kenya, Tanzania, Oman, Madagascar, Comoros, Mayotte, Ghana, Zambia, Uganda, Equatorial Guinea, Democratic Republic of Congo, Angola, Central African Republic, Burkina Faso, Malawi, Nigeria, Seychelles.

Some of the things are being able to toggle the colorscheme between colonizing countries and colonized countries, having tool tips appear for both maps at the same time, and colored arcs on the phylogenetic tree. Ultimately though, I wanted to be sure to convey the data in a comprehensive and coherent manner that fascilitates a further conversation. Therefore, I opted to prioritize that over the visuals being more robust perhaps. I believe that these visuals do a good job of telling that story and drawing a connection between the Scramble for Africa, Cassava-Infecting Geminiviruses, and Hospital Infrastructure.
